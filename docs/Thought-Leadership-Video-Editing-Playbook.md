# Thought Leadership Video Editing Playbook

## What a Thought Leadership Video Is (Editor’s Lens)

A thought leadership video is not entertainment-first. It is **authority construction**.

Goal hierarchy:

1. Establish credibility fast
2. Deliver a clear, valuable idea
3. Make the viewer feel smarter
4. Create trust and familiarity
5. Drive follow/engage/convert (soft CTA)

You are editing to make the speaker feel:

* competent
* experienced
* insightful
* worth listening to again

---

## Core Structure (High-Performing Format)

### 0:00–0:03 Hook (Authority Hook)

* Bold statement
* Contrarian take
* Insight preview
* Pain point framed intelligently

Examples:

* “Most AI products fail for one reason.”
* “If your team uses ChatGPT like this, you're wasting time.”

Editing:

* Start mid-sentence (no intro)
* Tight crop (face or eyes)
* Instant captions
* Subtle motion (zoom-in)

---

### 0:03–0:12 Context + Framing

* Who this is for
* Why it matters
* Problem setup

Editing:

* Add visual references early
* Introduce split-screen if relevant
* Keep pacing tight

---

### 0:12–0:35 Core Insight

* The main idea
* Explain clearly and visually

Editing:

* Use split screen (talking head top, visual bottom)
* Add diagrams, UI clips, examples
* Use text highlights for key phrases

---

### 0:35–0:55 Expansion / Proof

* Example, case study, breakdown

Editing:

* B-roll or screen recordings
* Annotate visuals (arrows, boxes)
* Maintain rhythm (cut every 1–3s)

---

### 0:55–1:10 Takeaway

* Simple, repeatable idea

Editing:

* Clean frame
* Less visual noise
* Strong caption emphasis

---

### 1:10–1:20 Soft CTA

* Follow for more
* Try this method
* Link in bio

Editing:

* Subtle animation
* No aggressive sales tone

---

## Visual Layout Systems

### 1. Split Screen (High Performance)

Top: Talking head
Bottom: Visual reference

Rules:

* Keep top 40–50% for face
* Bottom for supporting visuals
* Never clutter both areas
* Sync visuals with speech

---

### 2. Full Screen Talking Head + Cutaways

* Default state = face
* Cut to visuals when needed

---

### 3. Dynamic Zoom System

* Micro zooms every few seconds
* Punch-ins for emphasis

---

### 4. Caption-Led Layout

* Large bold captions center or lower third
* Highlight keywords with color

---

## Captions (Critical)

Style:

* Large, readable
* Word-by-word or phrase-by-phrase
* Highlight important words

Example:
THIS is why your ads are FAILING

Guidelines:

* Keep within safe mobile area
* Use 1–2 font styles max
* Maintain contrast

---

## Audio Design

Voice:

* Clean, present, no echo
* Slight compression
* EQ for clarity

Music:

* Low volume
* Subtle corporate/tech tone
* Never compete with voice

Sound Design:

* Minimal but effective
* Light clicks, whooshes, emphasis hits

---

## Pacing

Thought leadership ≠ slow.

Use:

* Tight cuts
* Strategic pauses
* Momentum without chaos

Rule:
Cut anything that doesn’t add clarity or value.

---

## Visual Support Layer

Use visuals to:

* Prove
* Explain
* Demonstrate

Examples:

* Screen recordings
* Charts
* UI demos
* Before/after
* Code snippets

Never show irrelevant footage.

---

## Authority Signals (Subtle but Powerful)

Add:

* Clean lighting
* Neutral background
* High-quality camera framing
* Professional typography
* Consistent branding

Avoid:

* Messy backgrounds
* Over-stylized effects
* Meme energy

---

## AI Talking Head Considerations

If using AI-generated avatars:

* Keep movements natural
* Avoid uncanny timing
* Sync lips perfectly
* Use realistic lighting and shadows

Compensate with:

* Strong captions
* Visual overlays
* B-roll support

---

## Retention System

Every 2–4 seconds change something:

* Crop
* Caption emphasis
* Visual
* Motion

This keeps cognitive engagement high.

---

## Common Mistakes

* Too slow intro
* Overcrowded screen
* Weak captions
* No visual support
* Flat audio
* No hook
* No takeaway

---

## Editing Stack Priority

1. Clarity of idea
2. Hook strength
3. Captions
4. Pacing
5. Visual support
6. Audio quality
7. Branding
8. Effects

---

## Pro Workflow (How Elite Editors Build These)

1. Script breakdown

   * Identify hook, insight, proof, CTA

2. Build rough cut (talking head only)

   * Focus on timing and clarity

3. Add captions

   * Sync with emphasis

4. Insert visuals

   * Only where they add value

5. Add motion system

   * Zooms, cuts, transitions

6. Audio polish

   * Voice first, music second

7. Final pass

   * Remove all dead space

---

## Export Checklist

* Hook strong in first 2 seconds?
* Clear without sound?
* Visuals aligned with speech?
* No clutter?
* Captions readable on mobile?
* Authority feel?
* Clean ending?

---

## Final Principle

A thought leadership video should make the viewer think:

“I learned something valuable from this person.”

If that happens consistently, authority compounds.
